Case study

Expert

A responsive web app concept that connects advice-seekers with experienced professionals for quick, paid 1:1 sessions. The goal: make discovery and booking feel fast, trustworthy, and calm.

Role

UX/UI Designer (student project)

Focus

Research → flows → wireframes → hi-fi UI → usability testing

Outcome

Clearer navigation, stronger hierarchy, and improved trust signals

Why this project

People often need practical guidance fast, but finding the right person (and trusting them) is hard. I designed Expert to reduce uncertainty with clear profiles, strong credibility cues, and a booking flow that feels effortless.

Process

Research & synthesis

I mapped recurring needs around trust, speed, and clarity; then turned them into design opportunities to guide the UI.

Personas

Two key user types shaped the tone and the features: a portfolio improver (Emma) and a career switcher (Daniel).

User flows

I defined three core flows (onboarding, post-session review, and booking) to keep the experience focused and testable.

Wireframes → UI

I progressed from low and mid fidelity to a consistent, card-based UI system that supports scanning and quick actions.

Testing & iteration

Peer feedback

Feedback highlighted clarity issues (icons, hierarchy, and time zones) and validated the calm tone and trust cues.

Availability clarity

I increased contrast and reduced ambiguity when experts are unavailable, so status is readable at a glance.

Loading state iteration

I added a more personable loading state to reduce perceived wait time and keep the app’s supportive tone consistent.

Usability testing

I captured patterns across participants and translated them into a short list of prioritized fixes and next steps.

After-session screen

Post-session actions were designed to make follow-up easy: review, save notes, and move to the next step.

Conclusion

Expert became strongest when the UI leaned into clarity: fewer decisions per screen, better labels, and trust signals placed exactly where people hesitate. Next step would be validating the booking flow with a higher fidelity prototype and real scheduling constraints.
